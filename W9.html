Capstone Project Week 9


Team work

This week we decided to complete our MVP (minimum viable product). After changing the core content of our game, we would like to have our workflow in the way as our mentor Matt suggested.

Stel had strong interest in building the MVP, so I decided to help set up the Jenkins system and add some fun features into our game. I discussed it with Stel and we agreed to add a mouse popping up feature in the game to “annoy” the players. I thought it was a very interesting idea so I couldn’t wait to start working on it.


My work

Before the Jenkins computers were set ready by the IT department, I had studied some materials on Google and some tutorials videos on Youtube. I thought I finally had a chance to work on Jenkins, and I was ready to put what I had learned into practice, but soon I found out I knew too little about it. Thanks to the support from people who were also responsible for Jenkins from other teams and our professor James, I finally made it work. I have learned how to put in the right info into Jenkins and I will definitely dive deeper into studying Jenkins build script later.


After the Jenkins system was set up, I finally started working on the mouse popping up feature. I scripted functions to make sure only one single mouse will show up and the player has to collide it to eliminate the mouse in 5 seconds or there will be punishment, and once the mouse is eliminated, the next one will pop up randomly between one to ten seconds.



The punishment will be a canvas getting set active to block the player's view.


After the scripting work was finished, I started the game art part. I spent some time looking for inspiration on Google and then started drawing on Gimp. I enjoyed the process very much, and I was happy with the work I finished.
